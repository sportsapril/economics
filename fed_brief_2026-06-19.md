## 🏛️ What's New From the Fed

**FOMC held rates at 3.50%–3.75% (unanimous, 12–0) — June 17.** The Committee left the target range unchanged for the federal funds rate "in support of the Federal Reserve's dual mandate" and reaffirmed its policy of maintaining ample reserves. The statement struck a notably hawkish, stripped-down tone.
- Key quote (statement): *"Inflation remains elevated relative to the Committee's 2 percent goal, in part reflecting supply shocks that have driven price increases in certain sectors, including energy. The Committee will deliver price stability."* The statement also flagged that elevated uncertainty "owes, in part, to the conflict in the Middle East."
- Source: [Federal Reserve — FOMC statement, June 17, 2026](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm)

**The dot plot flipped to a hike — June 17 SEP.** The median policymaker now expects the federal funds rate to **end 2026 at ~3.8%**, up from 3.4% in the March projections — implying at least one hike rather than a cut. Participants split: roughly **9 anticipate at least one hike, 8 see no change, 1 sees a cut**, and **17 of 18** judged inflation risks tilted to the upside. The Fed raised its 2026 inflation outlook to **3.6% headline / 3.3% core**.
- Source (commentary): [CNBC — Fed holds rates steady, June 17, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html); [StockTitan — "Fed Holds Rates June 2026; Dot Plot Flips to a Hike"](https://www.stocktitan.net/articles/fed-rate-decision-june-17-2026)

**Warsh's first meeting: forward guidance dropped, task forces announced.** New Chair Kevin Warsh emphasized an "unanimous, unambiguous" commitment to restoring 2% inflation, declined to revisit the 2% target, and spent much of the press conference detailing new internal task forces (communications, balance sheet, data, productivity/jobs, and the inflation framework).
- Key quote: *"The commitment to deliver is strong, unanimous, and unambiguous, and that's I think an important message we've missed for five years."*
- Source (commentary): [Fortune — Warsh's first Fed meeting, June 17, 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/06/17/kevin-warsh-first-fed-meeting-rates-steady-forward-guidance-dropped/); [CNBC — Warsh's task forces analysis](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/kevin-warsh-fed-interest-rates-risk-analysis.html)

## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

**Decisively hawkish — the most hawkish FOMC communication in this cycle.** The signal is in the specifics, not the on-hold decision:
- The **dot-plot median moved from a cut (March) to a hike (June)** — a full directional reversal in one quarter, with the 2026 year-end median jumping from 3.4% to 3.8%.
- **17 of 18 participants** see inflation risks tilted to the upside; the 2026 inflation projection was raised to **3.6% headline / 3.3% core**.
- The statement's language hardened: an unconditional **"The Committee will deliver price stability"** replaced softer, data-dependent phrasing, and Warsh stripped out explicit forward guidance.
- The driver is the **energy-led inflation re-acceleration** tied to the Middle East/Iran conflict (headline CPI back to 4.2%), which has overtaken the prior easing bias. Warsh's repeated framing — *"inflation is a choice"* — underlines an inflation-first posture.

The one offsetting dovish element is that the policy rate itself is unchanged and a July hold remains near-certain; the hawkishness is about the *path and risk distribution*, not an imminent hike.

- **Initial equity reaction was modestly negative.** On June 17 the S&P 500 fell ~0.6% and the Dow closed down ~1.0% (−507 pts) as the hawkish dot plot repriced a "higher-for-longer" path (commentary: [TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-17-2026), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html)).
- **Rate-sensitive / long-duration sectors face the most pressure.** A higher-for-longer rate path with 10Y near 4.46% is, per market commentary, a headwind for high-multiple growth/tech, real estate (REITs), utilities, and other bond-proxy sectors most sensitive to discount rates.
- **Value and "real-economy" cyclicals relatively favored.** Energy benefits directly from the supply shock driving the inflation story; financials can benefit from a positively sloped curve (2s10s +27 bps) and elevated front-end rates. This argues, in commentary terms, for a value-over-growth tilt versus the prior cut-driven setup.
- **Bonds: front-end repricing, curve still positive.** The 2-year's ~11 bps jump shows the market absorbing the removal of near-term cut hopes; the 10Y rose less, keeping a modestly positive 2s10s slope — i.e., not (yet) signaling recession via inversion.
- **A new-Chair credibility theme.** Several outlets frame Warsh's emphatic 2% recommitment and dropped forward guidance as a deliberate effort to re-anchor inflation expectations; the trade-off flagged in commentary is reduced policy-path transparency, which can raise day-to-day equity and rates volatility around data releases.
