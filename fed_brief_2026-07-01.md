3. **Warsh at Sintra (June 30):** framed inflation as the dominant problem, called the price-stability commitment unanimous, and left the door open to hikes if inflation persists. The removal of forward guidance is itself a hawkish-leaning regime change: it keeps optionality to tighten and discourages markets from pricing cuts.

Net: the Fed under Warsh is signaling it is more willing to *raise* than *cut*, with an inflation problem (core PCE 3.4%, headline CPI 4.2%) running against a still-steady labor market (4.3% unemployment).
