## 🏛️ What's New From the Fed

**Kashkari (Minneapolis Fed) flips from a cut to a hike — the headline development of the window.** Speaking Friday, June 26 at the Aspen Ideas Festival and in a same-day Bloomberg interview, Neel Kashkari — long one of the FOMC's more dovish members — said he now pencils in **one rate increase in 2026**, reversing the one cut he had projected in March. Key quote: *"In March, I had penciled in one rate cut by the end of the year. In June, I've changed that to one rate hike by the end of the year."* He attributed the shift to broad inflationary pressure, not just the Middle East: *"I'm concerned about inflation, and it's not only tied to what's happening in the Middle East, it's just the impression of broader inflationary pressures in the economy."* He cited tariffs on goods, fertilizer disruption tied to the Strait of Hormuz, and energy/oil prices. Significance: a known dove moving to the hawkish side suggests the inflation worry is broadening inside the Committee.
