**Governor Waller speech, July 6 (Rome).** Governor Christopher Waller delivered remarks titled *"Two Thoughts on the Transmission of Monetary Policy"* at a Bank of Italy-sponsored conference. This is a methodological/academic speech rather than a near-term policy signal: (1) he argued "initial conditions are crucial" to setting policy — a call-back to how the 2022 tightening cycle behaved differently than historical models predicted because starting conditions (job vacancies, fiscal stimulus) were unusual; (2) on forward guidance, he said it "is more art than science" and can help or hinder transmission depending on how rigidly it's applied, and floated that "in some cases, it's best not to use it at all" — consistent with the Warsh-era Fed's move away from explicit forward guidance.
