## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

**Decidedly hawkish, and unusually so for a hold.** The signal is in the specifics, not the tone:

- The **dot-plot flip** is the clearest evidence: the median 2026 path moved from implying cuts (March) to implying a **hike** (3.4% → 3.8% median), with the PCE projection revised up nearly a full point to ~3.6%. A Committee penciling in *higher* rates while holding is telling markets the next move is more likely up than down.
- **Statement wording** anchors inflation as "elevated" and explicitly ties price pressure to **energy/Middle East supply shocks**, framing the risk as upside-to-inflation rather than downside-to-growth.
- **Warsh's language** — "persistently high prices are a burden," 2% target reconsideration only "would follow its fulfillment" — reads as a deliberate credibility signal from a new chair, removing near-term easing from the table. Commentators characterized June 17 as the worst "Fed day" for a new chair since 1994 (commentary).

The one offsetting, *dovish-for-risk-assets* development is exogenous: the **U.S.–Iran framework to end the war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz** (signed June 18), which pulled oil and energy stocks lower and eases the very supply-shock channel the Fed flagged. If sustained, lower energy prices could soften the headline-inflation impulse that underpinned the hawkish dots.
