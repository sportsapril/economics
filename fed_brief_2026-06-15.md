## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

**Net: hawkish drift, anchored to the data rather than to any new Fed words (there are none — blackout).** The macro mix has flipped against easing:
- Inflation is **re-accelerating**, not cooling: headline CPI 4.2% (3-yr high), core 2.9%, core PCE 3.3% — all moving the wrong way and well above 2%.
- The **labor market is firm** (4.3% unemployment, +172k payrolls, upward prior revisions), removing the "weak jobs" justification for cuts.
- That is the textbook setup for *holding or hiking*, not cutting. The market's July odds (≈15% hike vs. <1% cut) reflect exactly this re-pricing.

The specific wording to watch Wednesday: whether the FOMC **keeps, softens, or drops** the "additional adjustments" sentence, and whether the **2026 dot** moves up (fewer/no cuts penciled in, or a hike). A higher median dot plus deletion of the easing-tilt sentence would confirm the hawkish read. The energy-driven nature of the CPI spike (Middle East supply shock) is the dovish counter-argument — the Fed often "looks through" supply-side energy shocks if inflation *expectations* stay anchored.
