## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

**Hawkish tilt, anchored to specific changes, not vibes:**

- The June 17 statement was **notably shorter** than prior statements and dropped language that had signaled a future easing bias. It closed with an unusual, deliberate line given the Fed's dual mandate: *"The Committee will deliver price stability"* — a choice to foreground the inflation side of the mandate over full employment. [FOMC statement text](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm)
- The **dot plot split 9–8–1**: nine officials project at least one hike by year-end, eight see no change, one sees a cut. New Chair **Kevin Warsh submitted no projection at all** — the first time a sitting chair has done so since the dot plot began in 2012, underscoring his stated preference to move away from explicit forward guidance.
- Waller's July 6 speech (above) reinforces the "less forward guidance, judged meeting-by-meeting" framing, without itself taking a hawkish or dovish position on the next move.
- This is a genuine two-sided read among reputable analysts, not a consensus: **Bank of America** (June 22 note, commentary) expects three 25 bp hikes this year (September, October, December), taking the range to 4.25%–4.50%, citing core PCE nearing 3.5% and sticky housing/services inflation. **Alpine Macro's Chen Zhao** (commentary) argues actual tightening odds "remain very low," expecting the Iran-war oil premium to unwind (Brent toward $50–60/bbl) and disinflation to resume as AI-driven productivity and softening wages weigh on the outlook. [Fortune, June 22](https://fortune.com/2026/06/22/fed-rate-hikes-outlook-sticky-inflation-kevin-warsh-job-growth-oil-prices/)
- A live complication: the U.S.-Iran conflict escalated further July 7–8 (reported strikes affecting a commercial vessel near the Strait of Hormuz, oil prices firmer), an upside inflation risk that cuts toward the hawkish case if it persists.
