## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

**Hawkish tilt, but with an important nuance.** The headline narrative is hawkish: CPI at a 3-year high (4.2%), an energy shock from the Iran conflict, a new chair (Warsh) widely expected to drop the prior easing bias for an explicitly neutral stance, and Logan flagging that *higher* rates may be needed this year. The market has internalized this — a December hike is fully priced.

The dovish counter-signal sits in the **composition**: core CPI rose just 0.2% m/m (below the 0.3% forecast) and core y/y is 2.9% — far cooler than the 4.2% headline. The inflation surge is concentrated in energy, not broad-based. That is why Treasury yields barely moved on a "hot" print: markets read it as an energy/supply shock that has *not yet* spilled into underlying prices. The anchor specifics to watch next week are whether Warsh's first statement explicitly removes easing language and how the SEP dot plot shifts — any upward migration of the dots would confirm the hawkish read.
