## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

With the Fed in blackout, there is **no fresh speech-based sentiment signal**; the signal this week comes entirely from the data, and it is **hawkish-constraining**. Headline CPI accelerating to 4.2%, PPI jumping to 6.5%, and core PCE grinding up to 3.3% all point in the same direction: inflation is re-accelerating, largely on an **energy-price spike tied to the Iran war**, which removes any near-term scope for the Committee to cut. The market response is consistent — FedWatch shows essentially zero cut probability for June and the 2-year anchored at ~4.1%.

The genuine uncertainty is **qualitative and arrives June 17**: this is Chair Warsh's first meeting, and the **new dot plot** will reveal whether the median 2026 path shifts toward fewer (or no) cuts given the inflation reacceleration, and how a Warsh-led Committee frames the energy shock (transitory supply shock vs. broadening inflation). *Commentary:* Warsh has historically been viewed as inflation-hawkish and skeptical of an over-large balance sheet, which some analysts read as a tilt toward holding longer; at the same time there is well-documented political pressure for lower rates. Both framings are interpretation, not Fed guidance — the actual signal will be the statement language, the SEP, and the press conference.
