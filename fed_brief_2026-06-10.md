## 🎙️ Sentiment Read

The signal this cycle is coming from **markets and data, not from Fed officials** (who are in blackout). The read is **decidedly hawkish-leaning**, driven by:
- **Inflation reaccelerating to a 3-year high (4.2% headline).** Even though it is energy-led and core stayed contained at 2.9%, a "fastest in three years" headline removes any near-term case for easing and keeps the dot plot's risk skewed up.
- **The Treasury market is effectively pricing the Fed as behind the curve** — the 2Y near a one-year high and ~63% odds of an October hike. As Bloomberg framed it, the bond market's message to Warsh is that "rates aren't high enough."
- **The dot plot is the live variable.** The key question for June 17 is whether new projections push the first 2026 cut into 2027 (or even pencil in a hike) versus leaving a September window open. Warsh — who previously leaned toward easing on a restrictive-policy view — now has to reconcile that with hotter inflation.
- **Political cross-pressure:** President Trump publicly stated a rate increase would be wrong ahead of Warsh's debut, adding an independence dimension to the optics. (Commentary — [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/trump-says-fed-rate-increase-135215588.html))
